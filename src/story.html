The Story

Five takeaways from the data

A quick narrative walk-through of what the dashboard reveals, in the order a category manager would want to hear it.

1. Makeup and Skincare lead the category race

Together they account for the largest share of revenue, well ahead of Fragrance, Haircare, and Bath & Body — a signal for where marketing spend and inventory should concentrate.

2. Revenue moves in waves, not a flat line

Month-over-month revenue shows clear peaks and dips across 2023–2024, rather than steady demand — worth investigating against promotions or seasonal buying patterns.

3. A small set of brands carries outsized weight

L'Oréal and Maybelline outperform the rest of the field, suggesting brand partnerships and co-marketing could be prioritized around top performers.

4. UAE and UK punch above their weight

Regional revenue isn't evenly spread — these two markets stand out, hinting at where localized campaigns could pay off first.

5. Ratings stay steady across categories

Average ratings hover consistently around 4.0 regardless of category, meaning satisfaction isn't the differentiator — revenue gaps come from demand and reach, not product quality.
